Here are some informations of the seiminars that I (co-)organize.

Spring 2025

PKU Seminar (with J. Luo): Asymptotic stability of moving sine-Gordon kinks

Time & Venue: 14:00-17:00, every Wednesday; Zhihualou-313. Ongoing...

Syllabus: We aim to demonstrate the details of [Chen-Lurhmann] on the moving sine-Gordon kinks and discuss more applications of their techniques, which involve (1) spectral theory of the linearize operator, representation of the evolution; (2) distorted Fourier theory, nonlinear spectral distributions; (3) space-time resonance method: normal form transform, null structure of evolution and modulation.

Fall 2024

WHU Seminar: Geometry of wave equation Strichartz estimates

Syllabus: (1) traditional and bilinear Strichartz estimates, original proof by frequency approach, c.f. [Klainerman], Chapter 8-9; (2) tube decomposition, induction on scales, geometry of wave-packet interaction, c.f. [Klainerman-Rodnianksi-Tao].

Spring 2024

WHU Seminar: Dispersion of non-linear waves: geometry and frequency

Textbook[Luk]&[Wang]

Syllabus: (1) Sketch of linear results; (2) Energy estimates, local well-posedness and blow-up criterion; (3) Elements of Lorentzian geometry, vector fields and multipliers; (4) Klainerman-Sobolev inequalities, global existence for $n \geq 4$ ; (5) Good derivatives, null condition and global existence for $n=3$ ; (6) Review of Littlewood-Paley theory and oscillatory integrals; (7) Non-endpoint Strichartz estimates, GWP for defocusing subcritical NLW; (8) Space-time resonance, relation to null condition, c.f. [Germain]&[Pusateri].

Summer 2023

WHU Seminar: Paradifferential calculus and non-linear PDEs

Textbook[Danchin] Vedio ExtraNote

Syllabus: (1) Littlewood-Paley decomposition, Bernstein inequalities, Besov spaces; (2) Bony's paraproduct, paradifferential calculus; (3) Smoothness effect of heat flow, application for semilinear Schrödinger equations; (4) Fujita-Kato's global result of mild solutions to Navier-Stokes equations; (5) Transport equations, result from ODE, estimates of commutators; (6) Beale-Kato-Majda's blow-up criterion for Euler equations.
